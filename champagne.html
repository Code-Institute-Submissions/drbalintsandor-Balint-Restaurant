How to Taste Champagne

Steps for Tasting

  • Sight: Observe the color and effervescence. Note the bubbles and the wine's hue.
  • Aroma: Inhale the bouquet. Detect the nuances of fruits, flowers, and yeast.
  • Taste: Take a sip and let it coat your palate. Sense the flavors, acidity, and texture.
  • Finish: Evaluate the aftertaste and overall impression.
  • Pairing: Experiment with food pairings to discover complementary flavors.

Types of Champagne

Champagne Varieties

  • Brut: The most common type, typically dry with a crisp taste and fine bubbles.
  • Extra Dry: Slightly sweeter than Brut, offering a delicate balance of sweetness and acidity.
  • Blanc de Blancs: Made exclusively from white grapes (Chardonnay), known for its elegance and citrusy notes.
  • Blanc de Noirs: Made from red grapes (Pinot Noir or Pinot Meunier), presenting bold flavors and richness.
  • Rosé: Produced by incorporating red wine, offering a delightful pink hue and red fruit flavors.
  • Vintage: Reflects the characteristics of a specific year's harvest, often showcasing exceptional quality.

Tasting Notes

Popular Champagne Brands

  • Moët & Chandon: Known for their Imperial Brut, characterized by its apple and biscuit notes. Luxury Champagne House founded in 1743.
  • Veuve Clicquot: Famous for their Yellow Label Brut, offering a rich and toasty palate. Founded in 1772, known for innovative contributions to champagne production.
  • Dom Pérignon: Prestigious vintage champagne, often showcasing complexity and finesse. Named after a Benedictine monk, produced by Moët & Chandon.
